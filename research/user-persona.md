# User Persona: NYT Games Player

## Primary Persona: "The Daily Puzzler"

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25-55
- **Education:** College-educated
- **Occupation:** Knowledge worker, professional
- **Tech savviness:** Comfortable with apps and web

### Characteristics
- Enjoys word games and puzzles
- Plays NYT Wordle, Connections, Crossword daily
- Part of their morning/evening routine
- Values intellectual challenge
- Appreciates clean, premium experiences

### Goals
- Complete the daily puzzle
- Maintain their streak
- Share results with friends/family
- Feel clever when they solve it
- Learn new words occasionally

### Frustrations
- Getting stuck on obscure words
- Breaking a streak due to one hard puzzle
- Cluttered, ad-heavy alternatives
- Spoilers from social media
- Wanting help but not wanting answers

### What They Want from a Helper Tool
1. **Definitions, not answers** - Help them figure it out, don't spoil
2. **Quick access** - Get in, get help, get out
3. **Clean interface** - Matches the NYT aesthetic they love
4. **No signup/payment** - Free and easy
5. **Mobile-friendly** - Often play on phone

### Quote
> "I just need a little nudge sometimes. I don't want the answer handed to me, but looking up 16 words individually takes forever."

## Secondary Persona: "The Competitive Solver"

### Characteristics
- Tracks stats obsessively
- Compares with friends
- Wants to solve without help
- Uses helper only as last resort

### What They Want
- Historical puzzles to practice
- Stats tracking
- Minimal UI, fast access
- Spoiler warnings

## Design Implications

### Must Have
- Fast, clean interface
- Clear spoiler protection
- Definition focus (not direct answers)
- Mobile-first design
- Dark mode option

### Nice to Have
- Historical puzzle access
- Stats/streak tracking
- Share functionality
- NYT theme match

### Avoid
- Cluttered UI
- Forced sign-up
- Ads or paywalls
- Auto-revealing answers
- Slow load times
